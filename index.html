Who's LUISA VALERO?

BIOGRAPHY

Became a designer

I want to share you about my life experiences that how I became a designer. I grew up the only deaf person in my family and friends in Colombia. I consider necessary to relate a fact that has marked who I am. I was born normal and full hearing. However, when I turned two years old, became deaf due to meningitis. This was a great challenge for me as I grew up in Colombia, a country with less developed public policies towards people living with disabilities.

My life in USA

I struggled with my high school to understand classes because it didn’t provide my full needs. I only enjoyed a class that I painted different type of paints and learned history of Art. Therefore, I did not know what I identified myself with until 2009 after I graduated from high school and went to Gallaudet University to learn American Sign Language (ASL) and English in United States of America. In this University, I met the Deaf community and its culture.

Graphic Designer

After completing my ASL and English, my goal was to get a degree in fashion design at Parsons School of Design in New York City or Istituto Europe di Design in Milan, Italy. But, my path changed everything, when I took Studio Fundamental’s class at George Mason University. Through this institution that I discovered that I wanted to become a designer since I loved visual communication. I transferred to the Art Institute of Washington (AIW) from George Mason University. I graduated from graphic and web design major at AIW with a concentration in graphic design.
